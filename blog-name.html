How to Keep Cool Without Air Conditioning

Manassas, Virginia can be pretty hot in the summer, and let’s face it – air conditioning is the 8th wonder of the world. It’s changed our lives for the better, and we wouldn’t give it up for anything. However, there are other ways to battle the heat that are pretty darn effective. Whether you’re waiting on a new air conditioner, need A/C repair or you’re just embracing a new way of life, here is a list of ways to keep cool that you may not have known about.

Keep your blinds closed. A lot of unwanted heat comes from your windows. Since you don’t want a greenhouse situation on your hands, close your blinds, shades and curtains to save on bills and significantly lower temperatures.

Doors are key. During the day when the temperatures hit a peak, close doors to rooms you’re not using. This prevents hot air from circulating through the house and contains the cooler air you’ve generated in a smaller area.

Cool the sheets. We know this sounds strange, but dampening your sheets with a few spritzes from a water bottle will help you stay much cooler at night. You could also invest in a bed-friendly Chillow cooling pad to put under your head or feet. Lastly, make sure your sheets are cotton and not satin, flannel or polyester – your skin will breathe easier and sweat less.

Turn on your bathroom and kitchen fans. People sometimes forget that these fans actually pull from the hot air that rises after you’ve cooked or taken a shower out of your house.

Grill instead. You know how you hover over the blast of hot hair coming out of your oven door on cold winter nights? That’s because cooking inside makes your house warmer. In the summer, keep the extra 400 degrees where it belongs – outside. Plus, the taste of grilled food is the essence of summer.

Soak in the night air. If you notice that the temperature has dropped considerably by nightfall, take advantage and open your windows before bed. Tip: position fans so they are able to blow hot air out of your windows and draw cool air in. Don’t forget to close your windows back up before bed to block out the morning heat.

How to Maintain Your Air Conditioner

Performing proper maintenance and repair on your air conditioning (A/C) unit is the best way to ensure many years of good service. Now that summer is in full swing, you may experience trouble with your A/C unit if it hasn’t been properly maintained or undergone an annual inspection.

To help you stay cool during the heat, here are three things you can do to keep your A/C running smoothly this summer.

  1. Clean or replace your air filters. The air filters inside your home could be compared to what your nose does for your respiratory system. When the air filters “inhale” and capture dust, dirt and other contaminants, they need to be cleaned out before those items are sucked in and cause problems inside your unit. Changing filters as stated on the packaging of your particular filter brand is ideal; if you experience troublesome allergies, you may want to swap out filters more frequently. Clean filters can lower your heating and air conditioning system’s energy consumption between 5% and 15%.
  2. Clean the condenser. (Important safety tip: remember to turn off the power to ALL parts of your A/C unit before self-servicing!) The condenser is the part of your A/C unit located outside. Throughout the year, be sure to control weed, grass and leaf buildup around the unit. Once a year, clean the intake coils with a commercial cleaner, always being sure to follow directions. You will also want to gently brush dirt from the outside fins, being careful to not bend or break the delicate aluminum. Finally, do a visual check of the concrete pad to see if there is any cracking or settling and use a carpenter’s level to make sure the concrete is still level. Not comfortable doing this yourself?   3. Hire the professionals. Though doing what you can to clean and replace the accessible parts of your A/C unit is crucial for a well-running system, it’s also important to know your limitations. Allowing a professional technician to replace and repair the portions of your system that are inaccessible will reduce the risk of mistakenly damaging or unnecessarily replacing any part of your unit.

Do You Fear Your Air Conditioning Bills?

As the summer heat rolls in, you may be starting to panic about your upcoming air conditioning bills. The EPA reports that heating and cooling use up as much as half of the energy in your home, so it makes sense that you pay attention to the bottom line impact of air conditioning. After all, you’re just trying to make your home environment comfortable, but every time the unit kicks on, you’re envisioning those utility dollars increasing.

There are a few steps that you can take to make a difference without the help of a professional. With central air conditioning, you can start by making sure that you change your air filters once a month. Buy air conditioning filters in bulk to save money, add your monthly filter change to your calendar, and make it happen! Changing your filter really does make a difference. When dirt builds up on the filter, your system has to work extra hard to move the air through. A harder working air conditioning system translates to more energy used and higher utility bills. A nice clean filter allows your air conditioner to work efficiently, saving you money.

Taking the time to properly program your thermostat is another step that can save you cooling dollars. Most of today’s programmable thermostats allow for multiple settings per day with easy override options, so setting the thermostat to warm the house during periods of time when you are typically away from home should be a relatively simple solution with minimal problems. Some people avoid modifying thermostat settings while they are away for fear that it costs more to return the home to the desired climate after setting the thermostat back, but the US Department of Energy reports that this is a misconception. During the summer, if you keep your house warmer while you are away, there is a slower flow of heat into your home, resulting in air conditioning savings.

So, if you’re really worried about those A/C costs, take these first do-it-yourself steps, and rest assured that you’ve done your part.
